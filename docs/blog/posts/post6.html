Keyboard Shortcuts and Power User Workflow

Keyboard Shortcuts and Power User Workflow

Published: December 2, 2025

While the mouse works perfectly, power users can fly through even the largest diffs using just two keys: A to accept the current pending hunk, R to reject it.

How the Shortcuts Work

As soon as a diff loads, the first undecided hunk is automatically focused. Press A to accept it and instantly move to the next pending hunk. Press R to reject and move on. The Final Result updates with every keystroke — you see the merged file take shape in real time.

Why This Is Fast

Most reviews follow a clear pattern: accept most changes, reject a few outliers. With keyboard shortcuts you never move your hands from the home row. A large pull request with dozens of hunks can be reviewed in under a minute.

Advanced Tips

Combine shortcuts with the view toggle button for maximum speed: start in Side-by-Side to understand layout, switch to Inline, then hammer A and R until done. Use “Use as Base” between iterations without ever touching the mouse.

FAQ

Do the shortcuts wrap around?

No. When you reach the last hunk, pressing A or R does nothing until new changes appear.

Can I customize the keys?

Not yet, but A and R are the universal standard for accept/reject in many tools.

Do shortcuts work on mobile?

No, but mobile users have large, easy-to-tap buttons instead.

Two keys. Zero friction. Total control.
