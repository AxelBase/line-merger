Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy

Effective Date: December 2, 2025

The **Line-by-Line Accept/Reject Merger** is a **100% client-side web application**. This means **nothing you paste, upload, or merge ever leaves your device**. We believe privacy is not a feature — it is the foundation of this tool.

We Collect Absolutely Nothing

Because all diff computation, decision tracking, and result generation happen entirely in your browser using JavaScript:

  • **No text** you input is ever sent to any server
  • **No IP addresses** are logged
  • **No analytics**, cookies, or tracking pixels are used
  • **No usage data** is recorded — not even page views

Your base text, modified text, and final merged result exist only in your browser’s memory and localStorage (which is automatically cleared when you close or refresh the page unless you choose otherwise).

How Your Data Stays Private

Every feature — from drag-and-drop file loading to real-time hunk processing and “Use as Base” — runs locally. Even the diff algorithm (jsdiff) and all rendering logic execute exclusively on your device. When you click **Copy** or **Download**, those actions are handled by your browser’s native APIs with zero server involvement.

No Third-Party Services

We do not use external analytics, advertising, error reporting, or CDN-hosted scripts that could track you. The entire application is statically hosted (e.g., GitHub Pages, Vercel, Netlify) and contains only open-source code you can inspect yourself.

Local Storage Usage

The only data stored on your device is what you explicitly allow: your base and modified texts are saved in **localStorage** so they survive page refresh. This is optional and under your full control. Clearing your browser data removes everything instantly.

Security Responsibility

Since we never see your content, we cannot protect it remotely. You remain responsible for using the tool on a secure device and avoiding public or shared computers when merging sensitive files.

Children’s Privacy

This tool is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any personal information from children.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy if new features are added that change data handling (unlikely). Any changes will be posted here with a new effective date.

Contact

Questions? Open an issue on our GitHub repository — we’re fully transparent.

Your text. Your device. Your privacy. Always.
